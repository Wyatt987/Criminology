Harold Frederick Shipman — Cover & Introduction

A forensic, educational case study — professional tone, respectful of victims.

Understanding why individuals commit murder is essential to forensic psychology and to improving public safety. The case of Harold Frederick Shipman, a medical professional who used his position to kill patients, illustrates how authority and trust can be abused, and how systemic gaps allow prolonged wrongdoing. This site presents a factual, forensic account of Shipman’s life, the observed patterns in his offenses, the investigative steps that led to his arrest, and the subsequent reforms that reshaped medical oversight in the UK. Our aim is to inform and to identify lessons for preventing similar abuses of power in healthcare.
